Training and Workshops

Learn to do the analysis yourself — from someone who does it for a living.

Bioinformatics and computational biology should not be a black box. We run hands-on, practical training workshops designed for researchers, graduate students, and industry professionals who want to build real computational skills — not sit through slides. Every workshop is built around actual data, actual tools, and actual problems. Whether your lab needs a one-day crash course in 16S amplicon analysis or a structured multi-week program covering molecular dynamics from scratch, we design the training around what you need to know and what you will actually use.

Workshop Catalog

What We Teach

Seven topic areas spanning foundational computing skills through advanced multi-omics, molecular simulation, and AI-driven biology. Every workshop listed below is available as a standalone session or as part of a structured course. Prerequisites are noted on each workshop — most foundational workshops assume no prior experience.

Bioinformatics Foundations

Beginner

Linux and Command Line for Biologists

The essential starting point for anyone entering bioinformatics. File system navigation, text processing (grep, awk, sed), shell scripting, remote server access (SSH), and job submission on HPC clusters (SLURM). Designed for wet-lab researchers and students with zero prior command line experience. By the end, participants can navigate a Linux server, process files, and submit their own analysis jobs.

Beginner

R Programming for Life Sciences

Introduction to R for biological data analysis — data import, manipulation (tidyverse), statistical testing, and publication-quality visualization (ggplot2). Covers the core skills needed to independently analyze experimental data, generate figures, and perform basic statistical comparisons. No prior programming experience required.

Need publication-quality figures? See our Scientific Writing service →
Beginner

Python for Bioinformatics

Python fundamentals tailored for biological applications — scripting, data handling (pandas, NumPy), file parsing (FASTA, FASTQ, PDB), basic plotting (matplotlib, seaborn), and automation of repetitive bioinformatics tasks. Designed for researchers who want scripting capability beyond point-and-click tools.

(Meta)genomics and Amplicon Analysis

Intermediate

16S/ITS Amplicon Analysis — From Raw Reads to Publication

Complete hands-on workflow using QIIME2 and R: quality control, ASV generation (DADA2), taxonomic classification (SILVA/UNITE), alpha and beta diversity analysis, statistical testing (PERMANOVA, ANOSIM), differential abundance (DESeq2, ANCOM-BC), and publication-quality figure generation. Participants leave with a reproducible pipeline they can apply to their own data immediately.

Want the full analysis done for you? See our Metagenomics service →
Intermediate

Shotgun Metagenomics and MAG Reconstruction

End-to-end metagenomic analysis: quality control, read-based profiling (Kraken2, MetaPhlAn), assembly (MEGAHIT), gene prediction (Prodigal), functional annotation (DIAMOND, KEGG, COG), binning (MaxBin2, MetaBAT2, CONCOCT), bin refinement, quality assessment (CheckM), and taxonomic placement (GTDB-Tk). Covers both read-based and assembly-based approaches and when to use each.

Intermediate

Metatranscriptomics and Functional Expression Analysis

From raw RNA-Seq reads to differential expression: quality control, alignment, quantification, normalization, DESeq2/edgeR workflows, pathway enrichment, and integration with metagenomic context. Includes practical guidance on experimental design for metatranscriptomic studies.

Advanced

Multi-Omics Data Integration

Hands-on integration of two or more omics layers using mixOmics and related frameworks. Covers sparse PLS, canonical correlation, multi-omics factor analysis (MOFA), and network-based integration. Participants work with real paired datasets and learn to identify cross-omics signatures that no single layer can reveal.

See our full multi-omics analysis service →

Molecular Dynamics Simulations

Beginner

Introduction to Molecular Dynamics — Concepts and Practice

For researchers with no prior MD experience. Covers the fundamentals: what MD simulations are, when they are appropriate, how force fields work, what the outputs mean, and how to critically evaluate published MD studies. Includes a guided hands-on session running a simple protein simulation from start to finish.

Need your MD simulations run for you? See our MD service →
Intermediate

AMBER MD Workflow — From PDB to Publication

Complete hands-on pipeline using AMBER: structure preparation (PDB2PQR, tleap), parameterization (ff14SB, GAFF2), solvation, minimization, heating, equilibration, production MD (pmemd.cuda), and trajectory analysis (CPPTRAJ). Covers protein, protein-ligand, and glycan systems. Participants build and run a simulation during the workshop.

Intermediate

MD Analysis and Interpretation

Focused on post-simulation analysis: RMSD, RMSF, hydrogen bond analysis, PCA, DCCM, clustering, SASA, MM-PBSA/MM-GBSA, and free energy landscape construction. Participants learn not just how to run these analyses but how to interpret and present them for publication. Includes best practices for statistical robustness — replicate analysis and convergence assessment.

Intermediate

Molecular Docking — Theory and Practice

Structure-based molecular docking using AutoDock Vina and related tools. Covers receptor and ligand preparation, grid setup, docking execution, pose analysis, scoring interpretation, and post-docking MD validation. Includes discussion of docking limitations and when to trust — or not trust — docking results.

AMR and Resistome Analysis

Intermediate

Resistome Profiling from Metagenomic Data

Hands-on identification and quantification of antimicrobial resistance genes from shotgun metagenomic data. Covers CARD/RGI, ResFinder, AMRFinderPlus, resistance mechanism classification, and resistome diversity analysis. Participants learn to go beyond gene lists to ecological interpretation of resistome data — understanding the community context of resistance, not just presence/absence calls.

Need comprehensive resistome analysis done for your samples? See our AMR Profiling service →

AI and ML in Structural Biology

Intermediate

AlphaFold and Protein Structure Prediction

Practical workshop on using ColabFold/AlphaFold for structure prediction — when to use it, how to assess confidence (pLDDT, PAE), how to prepare predicted structures for downstream analysis (MD simulations, docking), and what AlphaFold cannot tell you. Includes hands-on structure prediction and critical evaluation exercises.

Learn to validate AlphaFold structures with MD — see our MD service →
Advanced

Generative AI for Molecular Design — Concepts and Tools

An overview of the emerging AI/ML toolkit for molecular and protein design: ESM-2 for sequence annotation, RFdiffusion for protein backbone generation, DiffDock for ML-based docking, REINVENT4 for de novo small molecule design. Covers what each tool does, when it is appropriate, current limitations, and how to integrate AI-generated outputs with physics-based validation (MD, FEP). Designed as an orientation for researchers who want to understand the landscape, not just use one tool.

Scientific Writing and Data Visualization

Intermediate

Scientific Writing for Computational Studies

Practical workshop on writing manuscripts, methods sections, and results narratives for computational biology papers. Covers common mistakes in MD and bioinformatics manuscripts, how to describe pipelines reproducibly, how to present figures effectively, and how to structure a paper for maximum impact. Includes live editing exercises using participant drafts (with permission).

Prefer we write the manuscript for you? See our Scientific Writing service →
Intermediate

Publication-Quality Data Visualization in R

Advanced ggplot2 and related packages for producing journal-ready figures — multi-panel layouts, heatmaps (ComplexHeatmap), ordination plots, network visualizations, and consistent color and typography theming. Participants bring their own data and leave with finished, publication-ready figures.

Reproducibility and Workflow Automation

Intermediate

Reproducible Bioinformatics with Snakemake and Nextflow

Building automated, reproducible analysis pipelines using workflow managers. Covers Snakemake and/or Nextflow basics, rule/process design, dependency management, containerization (Docker, Singularity), and version control (Git). Essential for any lab that wants their analyses to be reproducible, shareable, and scalable — from a single sample to a hundred.

Workshop Formats

Not every group has the same schedule, infrastructure, or learning objective. We offer six delivery formats — from a focused single day to a full semester module — and we adapt the depth, pace, and content to match your group's level and goals.

Structured, progressive training covering a broader topic area. Each session builds on the previous one, with assignments or practice exercises between sessions. Allows time for participants to practice, encounter real problems, and get support.

A workshop designed specifically for your group's needs — your data, your tools, your questions. We consult with the group leader to understand the skill gaps, design the curriculum, and deliver training that directly advances your ongoing projects.

Ideal for: Any group where the catalog does not quite fit. This is the fastest path from "we don't know how" to "we can do this ourselves."
Target Audience

Who Is This For?

Our workshops are designed for people with biological expertise who need computational skills — not for computer scientists. You do not need a programming background to get started. What you need is a research question and the motivation to solve it yourself.

Graduate Students (MSc / PhD)

Building computational skills alongside your thesis work. Whether your project suddenly requires bioinformatics and no one in the lab can teach you, or you want to strengthen your CV with demonstrable computational skills, our workshops give you hands-on capability — not just theoretical exposure.

Undergraduate Students

Getting your first exposure to bioinformatics, programming, or computational biology. Our foundational workshops (Linux, R, Python) are designed for complete beginners. Final-year project students working on computational topics will find the targeted workshops (amplicon analysis, MD simulations) directly useful.

Postdoctoral Researchers

Expanding your methodological toolkit. If your new lab uses tools you have not worked with before, or your project has shifted into computational territory, a focused workshop gets you productive quickly — without months of self-teaching from tutorials that assume knowledge you do not have yet.

PIs and Lab Heads

Upskilling your group as a whole. Instead of sending students to figure things out independently (and slowly), invest in structured training that gets the whole group to a baseline competence. Also valuable for PIs who want to understand computational methods well enough to critically evaluate their students' work and published literature.

Industry and R&D Professionals

Building in-house bioinformatics or computational capability. Our workshops are designed for non-computational backgrounds — you do not need a computer science degree to learn these tools. We train pharma, biotech, agriculture, and environmental science professionals on the specific analytical skills their projects require.

Academic Departments and Institutions

Integrating computational training into your curriculum or PhD program. We design and deliver complete modules that fit your academic calendar and learning objectives. We have experience aligning content with UGC and institutional learning outcomes, and can scale to full class sizes with TA coordination.

How We Work

From Request to Running Code

Every workshop follows a structured process that begins before the first session and continues after the last one. The goal is that participants spend zero time troubleshooting setup and maximum time learning — and that learning does not stop when the session ends.

01

Needs Assessment

You tell us what your group needs to learn, what their current skill level is, and what your goals are. We assess the gap and recommend the right workshop format, topic, and depth. If you are unsure what you need, we help figure that out too — a brief conversation is usually enough to map the right starting point.

02

Curriculum Design

We design the workshop content around your group's specific context. This includes selecting relevant datasets (ideally related to your actual research), choosing the right tools and platforms, calibrating the pace and depth to the participants' level, and preparing hands-on exercises with clear, measurable learning outcomes for each session.

03

Environment Setup

We ensure every participant has a working computational environment before the workshop starts — whether that means cloud instances, your institutional HPC, or local installations. No time is wasted on day one fixing setup issues. We provide a pre-workshop setup guide and test connectivity with participants in advance.

04

Workshop Delivery

Live, interactive, hands-on instruction. Every concept is immediately followed by practice. We work through real data, encounter real errors, and solve real problems together. Questions are encouraged throughout — not saved for the end. For multi-session courses, we provide take-home exercises and support between sessions so learning continues between meetings.

05

Post-Workshop Support

Every participant receives complete workshop materials — scripts, slides, datasets, and a reference guide they can consult long after the session ends. We provide a support window (typically two weeks after the workshop) for follow-up questions as participants apply what they learned to their own projects. For institutional clients, we deliver a summary report on topics covered, skill levels achieved, and recommendations for further development.
