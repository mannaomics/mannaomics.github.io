Student Programs · Mentorship

Training and Internship

Your computational skills start here — learn by doing real science, not textbook exercises.

Not every student has a computational mentor in their lab. We offer personalized, one-on-one training and structured internship programs for undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral students who want to build genuine bioinformatics and computational biology skills. Whether you need hands-on guidance for your final year project, a computational co-supervisor for your thesis, or a structured internship that gives you publishable research experience — we work with you individually, at your pace, on problems that matter. This is not a recorded course. This is mentorship.

Programs

What We Offer

Five programs designed for different stages of the student journey — from final year undergraduates through to doctoral candidates. Every program is built around your specific project, your institutional timeline, and your current skill level. The goal in each case is the same: your independence, not your dependence on us.

Final Year Project Support

2 – 4 months  ·  BTech / BSc

Structured guidance for undergraduate students undertaking computational biology or bioinformatics final year projects. We help you from topic selection through to final submission — defining a feasible project, setting up the computational environment, teaching the necessary tools, supervising the analysis, and reviewing the written report.

You do not just get a finished project — you learn to do it yourself. Every session involves hands-on work where you execute the analysis under guidance. By the end, you will have practical experience with real bioinformatics or computational tools, a completed project you genuinely understand, and the confidence to discuss it in your viva or interview.

Typical Project Areas

  • 16S amplicon analysis of environmental or clinical samples
  • Genome annotation and comparative genomics
  • Molecular docking and virtual screening studies
  • Protein structure prediction and analysis using AlphaFold
  • Basic molecular dynamics simulation of a protein system
  • Resistome profiling from publicly available metagenomic datasets
  • R-based statistical analysis of biological datasets

How It Works

  • Initial consultation to define the project scope and timeline (aligned with your institutional deadlines)
  • Weekly or bi-weekly one-on-one sessions (remote, 1–2 hours each)
  • Hands-on skill building — you learn the tools as you work on your project
  • Ongoing support via email or messaging between sessions
  • Review of your final report or presentation before submission

Dissertation Support

4 – 8 months  ·  MSc / Postgraduate

Personalized computational mentorship for Master's students whose dissertation involves bioinformatics, computational biology, or multi-omics data analysis. We function as an external computational co-supervisor — working alongside your institutional supervisor to ensure the computational component of your thesis is rigorous, reproducible, and defensible.

Beyond the specific tools and pipelines, you learn to think computationally — how to frame a biological question as an analytical problem, how to choose the right method, how to assess whether your results are robust, and how to present computational work in your thesis. These transferable skills matter more than any single tool.

Typical Dissertation Areas

  • Metagenomic community analysis and MAG reconstruction
  • Multi-omics integration (metagenomics + transcriptomics, proteomics + metabolomics)
  • Molecular dynamics simulations of protein systems (wild-type vs. mutant, protein-ligand)
  • Comparative genomics and phylogenomic analysis
  • AMR gene profiling and resistome characterization
  • AI-assisted protein structure prediction and functional analysis
  • Computational drug discovery (docking, virtual screening, FEP)

How It Works

  • Scoping session with you (and your supervisor, if they wish) to define the computational objectives
  • Regular one-on-one sessions (weekly or bi-weekly, remote)
  • Progressive skill building — each session advances both your skills and your thesis data
  • Analysis troubleshooting and interpretation support between sessions
  • Review of thesis chapters covering computational methods and results
  • Pre-defense preparation if needed

PhD Research Support

6 months to full PhD  ·  Doctoral

Long-term computational mentorship for doctoral students whose research requires bioinformatics or computational biology expertise that is not available within their lab or department. We serve as an external computational collaborator embedded in your PhD journey — from experimental design through publication.

PhD-level support involves deeper methodological engagement, independent troubleshooting capability building, multi-paper trajectory planning, and the expectation that the student will become independently competent in their computational methods by the end. We do not just help you get results — we help you become the person who can get these results without us.

What We Provide

  • Computational experimental design — choosing the right methods, planning the right controls, defining the analytical strategy before data is generated
  • Ongoing one-on-one mentorship sessions (bi-weekly or monthly, adjusted to project phase)
  • Advanced method training as the project demands — you learn what you need, when you need it
  • Troubleshooting and interpretation support for complex or unexpected results
  • Manuscript development support for papers arising from the computational work
  • Reviewer response support when computational methods are questioned

Co-Authorship Policy

Where our intellectual contribution to a publication meets ICMJE authorship criteria — conception or design of the computational work, or analysis and interpretation of the data — co-authorship is offered and agreed upfront. We are transparent about this from the first session.

For PhD students, the long-term aim is independence. Early phases involve heavy guidance. Later phases shift to consultation and peer-level review. You should finish your PhD able to do this work without us.

Research Internship Program

2 – 6 months  ·  Remote / Hybrid

A structured, project-based internship for students who want dedicated research experience in bioinformatics or computational biology. Unlike academic coursework, the internship is built around a real research problem — you work on a genuine scientific question, learn the tools by applying them, and produce an output that has real value: a report, a dataset, a draft manuscript, or a contribution to an ongoing project.

Who This Is For

  • Undergraduate students (pre-final or final year) looking for research exposure before committing to a graduate program
  • MSc students wanting additional research experience beyond their dissertation
  • Recent graduates preparing for PhD applications and needing demonstrated computational research experience
  • Students from non-computational backgrounds (microbiology, biochemistry, environmental science) who want to build bioinformatics skills through project-based learning

What You Get Out of It

  • Hands-on experience with industry-standard bioinformatics or computational tools
  • A completed research project you can describe in interviews, applications, and your CV
  • Certificate of completion from Manna Omics Solutions

  • A clear, honest assessment of whether computational biology is the right path for you

Our Approach

What Makes This Different

There are many ways to learn bioinformatics — tutorials, online courses, YouTube videos. What you cannot get from any of those is a mentor who knows your data, knows your project, and can answer your specific question in real time. Here is what makes one-on-one mentorship different.

Mentorship, Not Outsourcing

We do not do your project for you. We teach you to do it yourself, with support. Every session involves you at the keyboard, you making decisions, you interpreting results. We guide, explain, troubleshoot, and push you to think critically. The goal is your independence — not your dependence on us. The work you submit, you can defend.

Real Problems, Not Toy Datasets

Wherever possible, we work on real data — your thesis data, publicly available datasets relevant to your research area, or data from ongoing MOS research projects. You learn to handle the messiness, the failed runs, the ambiguous results that real science produces. This is what prepares you for a research career, not clean textbook exercises.

Flexible, Student-Friendly Scheduling

We understand that students juggle coursework, lab work, and personal commitments. Sessions are scheduled around your availability — evenings, weekends, or between classes. For internships, we agree on a weekly time commitment that is realistic, not aspirational. If life happens and you need to reschedule, that is fine.

Institutional Coordination

We are happy to coordinate with your institutional supervisor or department. For thesis support, we can participate in committee meetings, co-sign progress reports, or provide external supervisor documentation where your institution requires it. For internships, we provide formal enrollment and completion documentation that your institution can recognize.

How It Works

From Application to Independence

Every program follows the same five-stage structure — starting before your first paid session and finishing with documentation and a forward-looking conversation about next steps. No time is wasted on logistics during the sessions themselves.

01

Application and Consultation

You reach out with your background, current program, research area, and what you are looking for. We have an initial conversation — 30 minutes, no charge — to understand your goals, assess your current skill level, and recommend the right program or track. If you are unsure what you need, that conversation will clarify it.

02

Program Design

We define the scope, timeline, session frequency, and learning objectives together. For thesis and project support, this is aligned with your institutional deadlines and supervisor expectations. For internships, we assign a track and project based on your interests and our active research. Everything is agreed before sessions begin.

03

Onboarding and Setup

We set up your computational environment, share any prerequisite materials, and make sure you are ready to start productively from session one. No time is wasted on installation issues during paid sessions — we handle setup before we begin.

04

Regular Sessions and Progress

Weekly or bi-weekly sessions with defined agendas and progress milestones. Between sessions, you work on assigned tasks and flag problems as they arise. We track progress against the agreed timeline and adjust pace as needed — if you are ahead, we go deeper; if something is taking longer, we adjust. Progress is transparent at every stage.

05

Completion and Documentation

Final deliverables reviewed, certificates and letters issued, and a closing session to discuss next steps — whether that is submitting your thesis, applying for PhD programs, or continuing with MOS on a new project. Many former students stay in touch, and that door is always open.
